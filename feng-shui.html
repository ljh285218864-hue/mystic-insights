What Chinese feng shui looks at

Entrance & front door

In Chinese feng shui, the entrance is where qi – the “breath” of the house – comes in. We look at how easy it is for good energy, opportunities, and people to arrive.

Bedroom & sleep

Bed position, doors, windows, and what is around you when you sleep can affect rest, anxiety, and how “safe” your body feels at night.

Desk, kitchen & money areas

Work desk, stove, and certain corners of the home relate to income, stability, and how quickly money flows in and out.

Direction, layout & timing

Classical feng shui uses direction and time cycles (like “flying stars”) to understand which rooms are naturally calmer, stronger, or more sensitive in a given period.

Feng shui is spiritual and energetic guidance. It does not replace medical, psychological, financial, or legal advice.

What can feng shui help with?

Traditional Chinese feng shui has always been used for very practical things: sleep, health, family harmony, money flow, business, and when to move or stay.

Sleep, stress & health

  • • Difficulty falling asleep or waking up exhausted.
  • • Feeling “on edge” at home for no clear reason.
  • • A bedroom that never feels fully restful or safe.

Money, work & stability

  • • Income goes up and down but doesn’t stay steady.
  • • Feeling blocked with career progress or clients.
  • • A home office that drains focus instead of supporting it.

Relationships & family life

  • • Tension at home that feels heavier in certain rooms.
  • • Couples always arguing in the same corner / area.
  • • A home that feels “stuck” when you’re ready for change.

How I approach feng shui

Feng shui has a long history in China of being used quietly – to choose houses, open businesses, arrange family homes, and support health. My approach is traditional at the core, but translated into modern language and practical steps.

  • • I look at the real structure of your home or office, not just decor trends.
  • • I combine direction, layout, and timing, not just “lucky items”.
  • • I prioritize changes that are realistic for renters and busy people.
  • • I explain why I suggest something, so you can understand, not just follow.

What I don’t do

  • • I don’t try to scare you with “bad luck” or fatalistic language.
  • • I don’t tell you to rebuild your whole house or buy endless products.
  • • I don’t promise to make you rich or fix every problem overnight.
  • • I don’t replace medical, psychological, or financial professionals.

Feng shui is about stacking small advantages in your favor and reducing friction in your daily life. It works best when it’s combined with honest self-reflection and practical action.

Frequently asked questions

A few common questions from people who are curious about feng shui but don’t want anything extreme or superstitious.

Do I need to “believe” in feng shui for it to work?

You don’t have to force yourself to believe anything. Many clients treat it as a mix of traditional wisdom and environmental psychology. We make changes, you live with them, and you notice how your sleep, mood, and focus respond.

Will you ask me to buy a lot of objects from your shop?

No. Classical feng shui starts with structure, layout, and how you use space. Sometimes a few objects (like a solid headboard or a proper desk) help, but most suggestions use what you already own. My shop is optional, not required.

Can you work with apartments or rentals?

Yes. Many clients live in apartments, condos, or rentals with strict rules. I focus on adjustments that respect your limitations: furniture placement, room usage, what you see when you wake up and when you come home.

I’m not in the U.S. Can we still work together?

Yes, as long as you can send a floor plan or simple sketch and clear photos. All guidance is written in English. Time zones only matter if you choose to add an optional call.
